Rugby

I've played rugby since I was 16. I played underage with North Kildare and at senior level with the Garda Rugby Club (as a "civie").

I started out in the back line but joined the real men in the pack at senior level. I played second row for a long time before finding my favourite position as an openside flanker. At 6' 5" and 90 kilos, I'm a lazy prop's dream in the lineout.

I've recently hung up my boots due to a series of concussions but I still tog out occassionally and enjoy playing tag in the summer.

Programming

As my girlfirend would say:

"He's busy nerding in his command station"

I first said "Hello, world!" using Visual Basic at college. Programming and Engineering (of any discipline) go hand in hand. While others shied away from it, I embraced it. It enabled me to develop and carry out much more detailed and advanced calculations and simulations. I continue to utilise Visual Basic on a daily basis in what my colleagues refer to as "Excel Wizardry".

I naturally wanted to explore other programming languages. Having used an iPhone for many years, I decided to try my hand at developing iOS apps. It was a great time to do so as Apple had released their new Swift programming language. I had a few ideas for handy apps that would help us out in work. I published my first app, "Pocket TGD", to the App Store.

I was hooked.

My next project was "Pocket Papers", an app for Leaving and Junior certificate students in Ireland. It received extremely positive reviews from students and teachers alike. However, I was constantly asked "when will it be available for Android?".

It was time to learn some Java!

A mountain of textbooks, hours of youtube, troves of stackoverflow forums and one month later, my apps were available on the Play Store.

Now if only I could do this for a living...
