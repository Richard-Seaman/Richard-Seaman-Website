All of the building regulations and technical guidance documents, right in your pocket.

I'll get back to you on that...

Nobody knows ALL of the building regulations and technical guidance documents off by heart. Well maybe some do, but they need to get out more! Sometimes, situations come up where you can't quite remember the specifics of a particular requirement. If one of these situations happens to be a question directed towards you during a meeting, the response is usually "I'll check that out in the office and get back to you".

Most of the time this is fine, you can simply look up the relevant material when you're back at the office and send off an email with your response. However, wouldn't it be more efficient to be able to respond there and then, putting the issue to bed and removing one of your required actions from the meeting's minutes.

You could always carry around hardcopies of all the documents you might possibly need. Although you may raise a few eyebrows when you walk into a meeting pushing a hand truck laden with boxes. Better yet, if the documents are available online you could try to find the right website and sieve through the documents until you find the one you're after.

Surely there's a better way?

Enter, Pocket TGD

I decided to develop Pocket TGD to help myself and my colleagues. The aim was to provide quick, easy access to reference material, no matter where the user is. I explain and demonstrate the concept within the video provided. (Pocket TGD was my first app, I was pretty happy to see it on the App Store!)

Pocket TGD is aimed particularly at engineers, architects, project managers and other professionals involved in the design and construction of schools in Ireland. It brings together content from a number of government bodies into one central, simple index.

The app was an instant hit. We were able to resolve issues much more quickly on site and in meetings. No more having to check things later. Naturally, other design team members and contractors started to use the app too. It is free to download after all.

iOS

The iOS version of Pocket Papers was launched in April 2015. It includes all of the Department of the Environment's Building Regulations and all of the Department of Education and Skills' technical guidance documents and room layouts. It also includes a handy daylight calculator to give a quick estimation of the daylight factor achieved, given particular room dimensions.

The available content does not change very often. The app's data is static. Everything that's available is included with the download of the app and any subsequent content must be released as an update. Although this increases the download size of the app, it ensures that the user has all the material they need without having to rely on an internet connection. This is particularly useful as many construction sites do not have reliable data coverage.

Android

The Android version of Pocket TGD was launched in April 2016. In the year between the iOS and Android launches, I was busy focusing on my other app, Pocket Papers. The Android version includes all of the same content as the iOS version.

I decided to make the content dynamic in this version. Partly because I wanted to keep the file size down and partly because I just wanted an extra challenge.

The content would be downloaded as required. Once downloaded, it would be saved for offline viewing. I could have hosted the content myself but I wanted to keep Pocket TGD as a free app (so paid hosting would be unsustainable). Therefore, I needed to use the official sources; the respective government websites. However, I didn't want to hard code the links into the app as third party content and websites are subject to change at any time. I wanted to be able to quickly adjust the links without needing to release an update.

As a compromise, I hosted a single JSON text file on one of my other active domains. This JSON file outlined all of the available content and the associated links (it was also included within the app to provide a default dataset). If there was ever a change or new content became available, I could update the JSON file and the user colud simply sync their app and the content would be updated.
